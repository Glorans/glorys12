Masterpieces of Italian Art

The Last Supper – Leonardo da Vinci

Origin: Milan, Italy

This masterpiece, painted on the wall of a convent in Milan, captures the dramatic moment when Jesus announces that one of his disciples will betray him. It's famous for its emotional depth and perfect use of perspective.

The Birth of Venus – Sandro Botticelli

Origin: Florence, Italy

Painted in Florence, this elegant work shows Venus, the goddess of love, emerging from the sea on a shell. It symbolizes divine beauty and was a major symbol of the Italian Renaissance.

David – Michelangelo Buonarroti

Origin: Florence, Italy

Carved from a single block of marble, Michelangelo's David stands as a powerful symbol of strength and human perfection. Created in Florence, it's one of Italy's most admired sculptures.

The Creation of Adam – Michelangelo Buonarroti

Origin: Vatican City, Italy

Part of the Sistine Chapel ceiling in Rome, this fresco shows God giving life to Adam with just a touch. It's one of the most iconic images ever painted, and a peak of Italian Renaissance art.

The School of Athens – Raphael

Origin: Vatican City, Italy

Painted in the Vatican, this fresco features ancient philosophers like Plato and Aristotle. It represents the harmony of art, philosophy, and science – core values of Italy's Renaissance.
